DNA data storage

DNA is a possibility, and almost a reality in the real world. Scientists have been storing data on strands of DNA since 2012, when harvard encoded a 52,000 word book into DNA strands. This was done by translating the DNA alphebet (ATGC) into 1s and 0s. Using this method we could potentially store the entire internet on only a couple grams of DNA. DNA is also great for storage because it can be remarkably durable when kept in dry cool conditions. they can work for up to 500 years even in harsh conditions. Comparing to things like CDs floppy disks, and magnetic tapes which only last 1 to 2 decades. Thats an amazing thing. To store the data itself you could either translate the binary to base4 to support the As and Ts and the Cs and Gs. or you could say ATs equal 0 and CGs equal 1. and translate the binary to genetic code or vise versa.
